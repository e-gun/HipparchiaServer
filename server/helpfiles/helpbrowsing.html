The text browser can be opened in two ways.
  • You can manually enter something in the Author and Text boxes and then click on the Browse to... button:
  • Alternately you can click on the colored locus in a search result. It will change color if you hover over it. [The sample locus is not clickable.] [2]   Homer,  Ilias: Book 1, line 75
The browser should now display a set of text lines centered on your choice. The number of lines you see can be set via the Configuration options... button.

Line numbers will be seen every N lines or every time there is a 'level shift' such as going from Book 1, Section 1 to Book 1, Section 2. The N in 'every N lines' can be configured by editing config.py and restarting HipparchiaServer.

(Almost) every word in the browser is clickable. If you hover over the words they will change appearance. If you click them Hipparchia will attempt to open a lexical window for this word that will parse it and show the relevant dictionary entry or entries.

Clickable words are affected by the choices you have made in the options panel under Display color shifts texts inside of brackets that are... Colored bracketing produces markup syntax problems. The color syntax will win. But if you turn off the colors, you will regain the clicks.

The bottom of the browser window contains three buttons:

     Browse backwards (click or press the ← key)

     Close the browser (click or press the ESC key)

     Browse forwards (click or press the → key)
